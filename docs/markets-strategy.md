# Bavarian Art Markets — Strategy and Notes

*Christopher Rehm — Bavaria*
*Last updated: April 2026*

---

## Purpose

Organized markets are the cleanest path to legal direct sales in public.
The organizer handles the permits, the audience is pre-qualified (people
walk in already in buying mode), and the cost of entry is a stand fee
rather than a permit lottery.

This document covers the two market opportunities currently on the radar
in the Augsburg / Bavarian region, with notes on suitability and status.

---

## Why Markets Matter

Three things organized markets do that plein air visibility cannot:

1. **They legalize the transaction.** No Sondernutzungserlaubnis to chase,
   no risk of being moved on by the Ordnungsamt.
2. **They concentrate the audience.** Everyone walking past is, by
   definition, browsing — not commuting, not heading to lunch.
3. **They build local network.** Other exhibitors, organizers, and repeat
   visitors are part of the regional art and craft scene. Even when sales
   are slow, the relationships compound.

The risk is selecting the wrong kind of market — one where the audience
is browsing for craft and decoration rather than fine art. That mismatch
is real and matters. See the Schröder Märkte notes below.

## Market 1: Kunst & Design Markt Augsburg

### What It Is

A touring art and design market operating across multiple cities in
Germany and Austria — Munich, Vienna, Stuttgart, Linz, Nuremberg, Graz,
Augsburg, Innsbruck, Salzburg, and Konstanz. The Augsburg edition is held
at the old Posthalle at Plärrer.

### Why It Looks Promising

- Over 150 exhibitors across fashion, jewelry, art, photography, graphic
  design, and product design
- Audience is explicitly looking for original, handmade work — not
  mass-produced goods
- The market positions itself around discovery and direct contact between
  makers and buyers
- The Augsburg location at Plärrer is well-connected by public transport,
  with parking available
- Geographic spread of the tour means it could become a recurring channel
  rather than a one-off

### What I Don't Yet Know

- Stand fees and exact terms for exhibitors
- Whether fine art (watercolour, oil) is genuinely valued at this market
  or whether the audience is more design-oriented
- Whether the foot traffic actually converts to sales for painters
  specifically, or whether jewelry, ceramics, and product design dominate
- Application timeline for the next Augsburg date

### Action Items

- [ ] Contact via the exhibitor registration page on the website
- [ ] Ask specifically about fine art exhibitors at past Augsburg events —
      ideally get the names of one or two painters who have done it before,
      to talk to directly
- [ ] Get the stand fee and the application calendar for the next
      Augsburg date
- [ ] Decide based on the answers, not based on the marketing copy

### Status

**Active investigation.** Worth pursuing seriously. The combination of
"original work, direct buyer contact, multi-city tour" is structurally
the right shape. The only question is whether the audience matches.

### Reference

- Website: kunst-designmarkt.at
- Augsburg location: Old Posthalle at Plärrer
- Multi-city: also Munich, Vienna, Stuttgart, Linz, Nuremberg, Graz,
  Innsbruck, Salzburg, Konstanz

## Market 2: Schröder Märkte — Kunsthandwerkermärkte

### What It Is

A regional Kunsthandwerk (artisan craft) market organizer based in
Friedberg, running markets in:

- Augsburg (Kongress am Park)
- Schwabmünchen (Stadthalle)
- Inchenhofen (Arnhofer Stadl)
- Schwangau (Schlossbrauhaus)
- Neuburg an der Donau (Residenzschloss)

Multiple dates per year, with registration via info@schroeder-maerkte.de.
Registration for fall 2026 dates opens in April 2026.

### Status: ON HOLD

I have done Schröder Märkte before. It is **not the right venue for pure
fine art**. The audience and the exhibitor mix are oriented toward
Kunsthandwerk in the traditional sense — turned wood, jewelry, textiles,
ceramics, soaps, decorative items, seasonal craft. Watercolour and oil
paintings sit uneasily in that environment. The buyers are not, on the
whole, looking for wall art at fine-art price points.

### Why Keep It On the List At All

- The geographic reach (Augsburg, Schwabmünchen, Schwangau, Neuburg) is
  exactly the regional footprint I want for name recognition
- Even if direct sales are slow, the visibility to repeat market-goers
  in the region has compound value over time
- The organizer is a known quantity — the relationship exists, the
  application process is frictionless
- It is possible the right *single* event in the right season (e.g.
  Advent at a high-traffic location) could justify another attempt

### Conditions for Trying Again

I would consider doing a Schröder market again only if:

- It is a high-traffic event (not a quiet Sunday market)
- The location is one I haven't tried before, or one where the
  demographic might be different (e.g. Schwangau gets tourist traffic
  near Neuschwanstein, which is a different audience than Inchenhofen)
- I can present the work in a way that clearly distinguishes it from
  the surrounding craft offerings — not just paintings on a table, but
  framed work at gallery-like presentation, with clear pricing and
  catalog material

### Status

**On hold.** Not the right primary channel. Reconsider for a specific
high-leverage date if one appears.

### Reference

- Email: info@schroeder-maerkte.de
- Website: schröder-märkte.de
- Friedberg-based, Augsburg region

## What is Missing

Neither of these markets is structurally ideal for fine art sales at the
price points I work in (€50–€3,000). The Kunst & Design Markt is closer
but still skews toward design and craft. The right venues would be:

- **Vernissage / gallery openings** as a participating artist (relationship-based)
- **Open studio events** like Tag der offenen Ateliers
- **Art-specific fairs** rather than general design or craft markets
- **Hotel and restaurant placements** in tourist-heavy locations
  (Landsberg, Füssen, smaller Bavarian towns where galleries are scarce)

These are not "markets" in the sense of this document, but they are the
direct-sale channels I should be evaluating in parallel — they are likely
to outperform any general craft market for actual fine art revenue.
